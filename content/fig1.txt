[4] The Altar for sacrifice by the Idolatrous Priests standings before the Gods of Elkenah, and Libnah, and Mahmackrah, Korash and Pharaoh.

[9] The Idolatrous God of Pharaoh.

[10] Abraham in Egypt.

[11] Designed to represent the pillars of heaven as understood by the Egyptians.

[12] Raukeeyang. Signifying expanse or firmament over our heads; but in this case, in relation to this subject, the Egyptians meant it to Signify Shaumau, to or be highs, heaven; or the heavens; answering to the Hebrew word, Shaumahyeem.
